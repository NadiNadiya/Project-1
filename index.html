WELCOM TO

Having survived the Mongol Empire, WWII, and Chernobyl, Kyiv is the proud capital of Ukraine. Filled with theaters, museums, religious sites, modern buildings and ancient ruins, the city of Kyiv is the center of Ukrainian culture. The Monastery of the Caves, founded in 1015, and Saint Sophia Cathedral, founded in 1037, are both World Heritage Sites. The Museum of the Great Patriotic War, topped by the massive Motherland Statue, provides gorgeous views of the city below.

Architecture

Kyiv's architecture blends historical charm with modern innovation, showcasing a captivating juxtaposition of ornate Orthodox churches and contemporary skyscrapers.

Food

Kyiv's culinary scene is a vibrant fusion of traditional Ukrainian flavors and international influences, offering delicious dishes like borscht and varenyky alongside diverse global cuisines.

Shopping

Exploring Kyiv's markets reveals a treasure trove of unique souvenirs, from intricately painted pysanky eggs to handcrafted embroidery, providing a glimpse into Ukraine's rich cultural heritage.

Spots to visit

From the iconic golden domes of Kyiv Pechersk Lavra to the historic charm of Andriyivsky Descent, the city boasts a wealth of touristic attractions that celebrate its history, art, and culture.
